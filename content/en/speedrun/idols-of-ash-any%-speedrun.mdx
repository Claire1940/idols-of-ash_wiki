## Core Movement Mechanics

Speed in Idols of Ash is not just about moving forward; it is about maintaining momentum across vertical and horizontal planes. Unlike traditional platformers, the momentum in this game carries over between different states, such as jumping, sprinting, and grappling.

### Sprinting and Stamina Management
While it may seem intuitive to hold the sprint key at all times, top runners know that stamina management is vital. If you deplete your stamina completely, you suffer a recovery penalty that can ruin a run. Instead, use "sprint pulsing" during long horizontal stretches.

### The Grapple Hook
The grapple hook is the most important tool in your arsenal. It allows for massive skips, but it is also prone to "bugging out" if used too close to certain geometry. Experienced runners use the grapple to slingshot themselves around corners rather than simply pulling themselves upward.

## The Any% Route Breakdown

In a 2026 **idols of ash any% speedrun**, the goal is to reach the final trigger at the bottom of the map as quickly as possible. The run is generally divided into three major phases: The Ascent, The Transition, and The Great Descent.

### The Ascent
The first 90 seconds of the run involve a rapid climb. You should focus on high-angle grapples. Look for the "Iron Ribs" section—if you can skip the middle platform by grappling the overhead beam directly, you save approximately 4 seconds.

### The Transition
This is where the run is often won or lost. You must navigate a series of narrow corridors. Precision is key here; hitting a wall will kill your momentum and likely prevent you from hitting the optimal "Drop Point."

### The Great Descent
The final phase involves falling almost the entire height of the map. This is where the "Fall Damage Glitch" comes into play. By hitting specific slanted surfaces or utilizing a frame-perfect grapple just before impact, you can survive falls that would normally be fatal.

## Equipment and Optimization

For a high-level **idols of ash any% speedrun**, your hardware and in-game settings matter. Input lag is the enemy of the grapple hook.

1.  **Frame Rate:** Cap your FPS at a stable number (usually 120 or 144). Physics in Idols of Ash are tied to frame timing; fluctuations can cause your grapple to disconnect prematurely.
2.  **Sensitivity:** High mouse sensitivity is recommended for "flick-grappling," which is necessary for the rapid vertical transitions in the first half of the run.
3.  **Keybinds:** Rebind your grapple to a mouse button or an easily accessible key like 'E' to ensure you can maintain movement (WASD) while aiming.

> **💡 Tip:** If you find yourself failing the "sprint" transitions, check your stamina bar. Sometimes the game bugs and prevents sprinting if you were in the middle of a grapple animation. Re-pressing the key usually fixes this.
