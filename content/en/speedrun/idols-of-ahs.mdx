## Core Gameplay: Mastering the Descent

The fundamental loop of **idols of ahs** revolves around the grapple claw. This tool allows you to latch onto wooden beams, stone outcroppings, and even organic tree roots to swing across gaps or lower yourself safely. However, the game utilizes a realistic physics engine where gravity is your most persistent enemy.

### Grappling and Rope Management

Success in the descent depends on how you manipulate your rope length. While attached to a surface, you can reel yourself in to gain height for a better swing or let out rope to drop closer to a lower platform. Mastering the "Shift" key to lock your position or reel in quickly is vital when the centipede begins its sprint.

| Action | Control (Default) | Tactical Use |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Throw Grapple** | Left Click | Latch onto any valid surface within range. |
| **Reel In / Pull** | Hold Shift | Shorten the rope to avoid obstacles or gain swing height. |
| **Slide Down** | Control / S | Rapidly descend the rope to reach lower levels quickly. |
| **Jump Release** | Space | Release the grapple at the peak of a swing for maximum distance. |

> **💡 Tip:** Always secure your grapple before looking over a ledge. Safety 101 in this megastructure prevents accidental slips that lead to instant death or heavy fall damage.

## Surviving the Giant Centipede

The primary source of tension in **idols of ahs** is the unrelenting pursuit of the centipede. This creature does not simply follow a set path; it navigates the ruins with surprising agility, crawling along walls and ceilings to cut off your escape.

### Behavioral Patterns
The centipede emits distinct auditory cues. You will hear a chittering sound when it is at a distance, which transitions into heavy, rhythmic breathing as it closes in. When the creature reaches a certain proximity, it enters a "sprint" phase, lunging toward your current position.

*   **Human Mimicry:** The creature possesses human-like hands and a distorted face, adding a layer of psychological horror to the chase.
*   **Vertical Agility:** It can move faster than you on flat vertical surfaces. Your only advantage is your ability to swing into open space where it cannot follow easily.
*   **Ambush Points:** Certain narrow corridors and tree-root sections are designed as ambush zones. In these areas, speed is more important than precision.

## Vitality and Checkpoints: The Idols of Ash

Scattered throughout the descent are "Urns" or "Idols." These serve two purposes: they act as checkpoints and provide narrative fragments that explain the protagonist's motivation. Breathing in the ancient ashes triggers memories of "her" and a companion named Praxtos.

### Health and Recovery
While fall damage is a constant threat, you can recover vitality by finding "Latent Embers" or "Kilns." These glowing green or blue objects rekindle your health bar, allowing you to take riskier jumps.

| Item Type | Effect | Rarity |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Ash Urn** | Sets checkpoint and restores full health. | Rare (Story Beats) |
| **Latent Ember** | Small health boost. | Common |
| **Blue Kiln** | Significant health restoration. | Uncommon |

> **⚠️ Warning:** In Nightmare Mode, these Kilns are destroyed, meaning you must complete the entire descent without the ability to heal or respawn at mid-way points.

## Advanced Movement: Speedrun Tactics

For players looking to master **idols of ahs** in 2026, learning "Momentum Stopping" and "Blind Drops" is essential. Speedrunners often bypass the intended winding paths by jumping into the void and grappling at the last possible second to negate fall damage.

1.  **The Last-Second Catch:** Jump from a high ledge and wait until you are near the ground before firing your grapple. If timed correctly, the rope tension will reset your downward velocity.
2.  **Surf Map Sliding:** On sloped surfaces like tree roots, you can "surf" to maintain high horizontal speed. Use the grapple to steer rather than to stop.
3.  **Shortcut Navigation:** Look for "ghost structures" or faint outlines in the desaturated fog. These often indicate faster, albeit riskier, paths down the megastructure.

## The Story: Regret and Memory

The narrative of **idols of ahs** is told through the "Face of Regret." The protagonist is descending to find a woman who suffered from "Coil Rot." The dialogue suggests that the megastructure is a place of illusions, where the ashes remember those who enter. The ending is bittersweet, implying that the journey might be a form of grieving or a final visit to a grave.

### Narrative Milestones
*   **The Village Memory:** Recalls the day the protagonist left home.
*   **Praxtos' Plea:** A memory of a friend trying to dissuade the protagonist from the journey.
*   **The Coil Rot:** Reveals the sickness that took "her" and the physical toll it took on her body.
*   **The Final Idol:** The protagonist becomes "preserved ash," finally reaching the distant shape of their loved one.

## FAQ

**Q: Is there fall damage in idols of ahs?**

A: Yes, fall damage is a significant mechanic. While small drops are tolerable, falling from extreme heights will deplete your health bar instantly. You can use the grapple claw to break your fall and reset your momentum.

**Q: How do I escape the centipede when it starts sprinting?**

A: The best strategy is to swing into open air. The centipede is fastest when it has a surface to crawl on. By swinging away from the walls and landing on a distant platform, you force the creature to take a longer path to reach you.

**Q: What are the green glowing objects?**

A: These are Latent Embers or Kilns. Interacting with them restores your vitality (health). They are essential for surviving long stretches between checkpoints, especially if you take damage from falls or the centipede.

**Q: What happens if I miss a checkpoint?**

A: If you miss an Ash Urn, you will respawn at the last one you successfully activated. In Nightmare Mode, however, there are no checkpoints at all, meaning any mistake will send you back to the very beginning of the game.
