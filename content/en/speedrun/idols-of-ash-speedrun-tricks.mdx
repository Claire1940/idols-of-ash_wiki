## Essential Power-Ups and Movement Skips

A common mistake for new runners is attempting to collect every creature power-up available. In a competitive setting, efficiency is king. You only need a handful of specific abilities to clear the mandatory story gates. By ignoring optional powers like those from Drilbur, Zorua, and Lapras, you save nearly 45 minutes of unnecessary questing.

| Power-Up Source | Ability | Priority | Reason for Inclusion/Skip |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Squirtle** | Water Gun | **Mandatory** | Required to hydrate wasteland and grow essential trees. |
| **Bulbasaur** | Grass Growth | **Mandatory** | Necessary for spawning quest-specific creatures. |
| **Hitmonchan** | Rock Smash | **Mandatory** | Clears terrain obstacles and gathers early minerals. |
| **Scyther** | Cut | **Mandatory** | Required for clearing paths and harvesting lumber. |
| **Magikarp** | Jump | **Mandatory** | Provides verticality needed for shortcutting ledge paths. |
| **Lapras** | Surf | **Skip** | Most water sections are bypassable via jump or specific pathing. |
| **Graveler** | Speed Boost | **Skip** | The time taken to obtain the power outweighs the speed gains. |

> **Pro Tip:** Focus on "micromanaging" your creature's actions. While waiting for a quest NPC to move, use your Squirtle to water future crop sites or have Scyther clear trees you will need for the Team Initiation Challenge later.

## Optimizing the Withered Wasteland

The first major hurdle in any run is the Withered Wasteland. Most players struggle with the infamous "Slowpoke Yawn" quest, which requires making the environment humid enough to progress. One of the most effective **idols of ash speedrun tricks** involves manipulating the spawn location of Slowpoke.

Instead of following the game's suggestion to plant grass near the Magikarp spawn—which forces the Slowpoke to walk a long, winding path—clear a small walking path near where you first obtained Hitmonchan. By planting grass and watering the dirt at the rocky shore, you force the Slowpoke to spawn much closer to the Pokémon Center. This creates a straight, unobstructed path for the Slowpoke to walk back to Professor Tangro, saving several minutes of waiting.

During this waiting period, do not stand idle. You should be:
1. Collecting Leppa Berries for the first tier of the initiation challenge.
2. Building temporary "messy" habitats using discarded stairs and signs.
3. Spawning Timber and Charmander to assist with future construction.
4. Grabbing the Castform Rain Doll to trigger the Rain Dance, which is vital for freeing Onix from his clay prison.

## Bleak Beach and the Mosslax Skip

Upon reaching Bleak Beach, your primary goal is to satisfy the spoiled Meowth to progress the environment levels. Speedrunners avoid the crafting bench whenever possible, instead opting to pick up items directly off the ground—like glowing mushrooms and poles—to fulfill quest requirements.

The "Mosslax" (a moss-covered variant of Snorlax) quest is the centerpiece of this area. To wake it up, you need the Pikachu power-up. Efficient runners connect generators using a series of lights and poles found in the environment rather than crafting them. This powers the town and the nearby vending machine, which provides a massive boost to the environment level just by existing.

## Rocky Ridges: The Time Travel Exploit

Rocky Ridges introduces Chef Dente and the Power Meal system. This is where the run utilizes one of the most powerful **idols of ash speedrun tricks**: Date Skipping. The game’s engine does not punish players for changing their system clock, allowing for "instant" completion of tasks that would otherwise take hours.

### The Power Meal Cycle
To free Chef Dente from his barrel, you must craft a series of specific meals. By bringing creatures like Timber and Squirtle from previous areas, you can set up a makeshift kitchen immediately.

| Recipe | Required Tools | Speedrun Shortcut |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Power Salad** | Cutting Board | Use Dartrix to cut vegetables gathered in Area 2. |
| **Power Bread** | Oven | Spawn a Hoothoot immediately to manage the fire. |
| **Power Steak** | Frying Pan | Use the **Date Skip** to instantly smelt the iron needed for the pan. |

Using the Power Steak is essential because it buffs your Rock Smash ability, allowing you to break the "Fruity Pebble" blocks and Gold blocks required for the endgame. Without this buff, you would be forced to complete several optional side quests to upgrade your gear naturally.

## Rebuilding Silco in Sparkling Skylands

The final area, Sparkling Skylands, centers on Tinkaton and the reconstruction of the Silco building. The most efficient strategy here is to carry over your "band of monsters" from previous areas. While moving creatures between hubs slightly lowers the local environment level, it is far faster than spawning and befriending new ones in each zone.

To rebuild Silco rapidly:
1. **The Lift Skip:** You only need to place the lift for a single second to trigger the quest flag. Immediately pick it back up and move it to a more useful shortcut location.
2. **Floor Construction:** Use the minerals and ingots you farmed in Rocky Ridges to instantly build Floor 1. 
3. **NPC Recruitment:** Teleport back to previous areas to grab Chef Dente and Pikachu. Their presence is required for the final floors, and having them ready to teleport saves significant travel time.

Once the building is complete, you will receive the Master Ball. However, the run isn't over until you return to the Withered Wasteland to finish the Team Initiation Challenge.

## FAQ

**Q: Why do speedrunners skip the Lapras power-up?**

A: While Surf is useful for casual play, the time required to complete the Lapras questline is significantly longer than simply using Magikarp's Jump or Hitmonchan's pathing to bypass water obstacles. It is one of the most common **idols of ash speedrun tricks** used to save time.

**Q: Does the "Date Skip" glitch affect my save file?**

A: In the current 2026 build, date skipping is considered a "soft" exploit. It does not corrupt save files, but it will instantly refresh all resource nodes and complete any ongoing construction tasks.

**Q: What is the hardest part of the 3-hour run?**

A: The Team Initiation Challenge is the most difficult segment because it requires precise inventory management. Forgetting a single item, like the 10 Crystal Fragments, can force a back-track that kills the run.

**Q: Can I use any photo for the final quest?**

A: Yes. While the game suggests a "cherished" photo of an NPC, the quest trigger only checks if a photo exists in your inventory. Taking a photo of the floor or a wall is the standard practice for top-tier runners.
