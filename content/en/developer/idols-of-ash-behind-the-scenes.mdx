## Master the Descent: Movement and Controls

The core of the gameplay revolves around the grappling hook and vertical movement. Survival depends on your ability to swing, rappel, and climb with precision. Unlike many platformers, gravity is your greatest enemy here, and a single missed hook can lead to a lethal fall.

### Core Control Scheme

| Action | Input | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Shoot Grappling Hook** | Left Mouse Button | Attaches to specific wooden beams or rough rock edges. |
| **Zoom/Aim** | Right Mouse Button | Allows for precise aiming of the hook at distant targets. |
| **Rappel Down** | Ctrl | Lowers the player slowly while attached to a rope. |
| **Climb Up** | Space / Shift | Pulls the player upward toward the anchor point. |
| **Swing Movement** | W, A, S, D | Used to build momentum while hanging to reach distant platforms. |

To master the **idols of ash behind the scenes** mechanics, players should practice the "swing-and-release" technique. By building momentum with the WASD keys and releasing the hook at the peak of the arc, you can clear massive gaps that would otherwise be impassable.

> **💡 Tip:** Always look for the glowing "Life Embers" scattered on ledges. These act as health restores and are often placed near difficult platforming sections to provide a safety net.

## Surviving the Giant Centipede

The primary antagonist of the game is a massive, multi-legged horror that stalks the canyon walls. This creature represents the "predatory nature" of the abyss. It does not simply attack; it waits for you to be at your most vulnerable—usually while you are dangling from a rope.

### Centipede Behavior Patterns

1.  **Auditory Warning:** The creature makes a distinct skittering sound before it appears. If you hear this, find solid ground immediately.
2.  **Vertical Pursuit:** The centipede can move faster than you can climb. Use the rappel function (Ctrl) to drop quickly if it approaches from above.
3.  **Safety in Suspension:** Interestingly, the creature has difficulty targeting players who are actively swinging. Constant movement is your best defense.
4.  **Checkpoint Reset:** Reaching a "Hidden Ember" (save point) typically despawns the creature for a short duration, giving you time to breathe.

Managing the centipede is a psychological battle. The developers designed the encounter to induce "height anxiety," forcing players to look down into the void to track the creature's movement. This reinforces the **idols of ash behind the scenes** design philosophy of making the environment itself feel alive and hostile.

As the protagonist inhales the final batch of ash, they see the face of the person they lost. However, the physical toll of the journey and the toxic nature of the ash take their final price. Like the villagers before them, the protagonist turns to stone, becoming another "idol" in the ash.

## FAQ

**Q: Is there a way to kill the giant centipede in Idols of Ash?**

A: No, the centipede cannot be killed. It is an environmental hazard meant to be avoided. Your only defense is speed, agility, and reaching save points to reset its position.

**Q: What do the different colors of ash signify?**

A: While most ash is grey, certain "Primal Ashes" have a golden or blue tint. These typically signify major story milestones and provide longer, more detailed cinematic projections of the village's final days.

**Q: Can I save my progress anywhere?**

A: Progress is saved at "Hidden Embers"—small campfires found on ledges. You must interact with them to set your respawn point. If you die without reaching one, you will return to the last ember you lit.

**Q: Does the ending change if I collect all the ash fragments?**

A: The core ending remains the same, but collecting all fragments provides a deeper **idols of ash behind the scenes** look at the protagonist's motivations, adding more emotional weight to the final scene where they turn to stone.
