## Advanced Movement Tech for Nightmare Runs

Once you have learned **idols of ash how to unlock nightmare**, the real work begins. The movement physics in Nightmare are less forgiving, and the environmental hazards are more frequent. To succeed, you must master "Grapple Boosting."

### Grapple Boosting on the Floor
One of the most effective strategies discovered by top-tier players is floor-based grappling. While the game encourages verticality, staying low can actually be faster. Grapple boosting on a flat surface allows you to move at speeds that exceed the crawl rate of most pursuer bots. This is essential for maps where Kelly is putting on extreme pressure.

### The "Scary Bounce"
In Nightmare mode, you will encounter specific terrain pieces that provide a massive kinetic boost if hit at the right angle. Often referred to as the "Scary Bounce," this maneuver can shave seconds off your time but carries the risk of sending you into a death pit if your grapple isn't ready to catch a wall immediately after.

## Surviving the Pursuit: Dealing with Kelly

In Nightmare mode, the pursuer AI (Kelly) is significantly more intelligent. She will no longer follow a set path but will instead attempt to intercept your trajectory. This makes "free falling" through the map much more dangerous, as you are likely to fall right into her clutches.

> **Tip:** Use the terrain to your advantage. Kelly's pathfinding struggles with rapid vertical shifts. If she is gaining on you, a quick grapple-up followed by an immediate drop can often reset her pursuit timer.

### Inverted Nightmare Considerations
When you transition to Inverted Nightmare, the entire map is flipped. This doesn't just change the visuals; it changes the gravity-based shortcuts you may have relied on in the standard Nightmare mode. Inverted maps require you to recognize familiar landmarks from a completely different perspective, making your grapple placement even more critical.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I unlock Nightmare mode on my first playthrough?**

A: No, Nightmare mode is generally locked until you have completed the main story at least once on Normal or Hard difficulty. This ensures players have a baseline understanding of the grappling physics before facing the increased difficulty.

**Q: What is the difference between Nightmare and Inverted Nightmare?**

A: Nightmare mode increases AI speed, reduces health pickups, and tightens time limits. Inverted Nightmare includes all those changes but also flips the entire map geometry, forcing you to navigate the world upside down.

**Q: Is there a specific character I should use for Nightmare runs?**

A: While most characters are viable, those with higher "Grapple Acceleration" stats are generally preferred for Nightmare mode to help escape Kelly's increased pursuit speed.

**Q: Does idols of ash how to unlock nightmare require finding all hidden idols?**

A: While finding all idols is not strictly required for the basic Nightmare unlock, collecting a high percentage of them (usually 75% or more) is often necessary to unlock the "True Nightmare" achievements and certain shiny grapple skins.
