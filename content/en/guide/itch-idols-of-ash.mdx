## Core Gameplay: The Art of the Descent

The primary mechanic in **itch idols of ash** is the grapple claw. This tool is your lifeline, allowing you to swing across massive gaps, lower yourself safely into pits, and bypass dangerous terrain. Mastery of the physics engine is essential because the game does not hold your hand. Momentum, rope length, and attachment points all factor into your survival.

### Grapple Claw Mechanics

The grapple claw functions through a three-step process: throwing, anchoring, and reeling. Understanding how to manipulate the rope length while swinging is the difference between a graceful landing and a fatal fall.

| Action | Input/Effect | Strategic Use |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Throw Grapple** | Primary Action | Aim for solid stone or wooden roots; avoid smooth, angled surfaces. |
| **Pull in Rope** | Shorten Length | Use this to gain height mid-swing or to tighten your arc for precision. |
| **Release Rope** | Lengthen / Drop | Essential for "rappelling" down long vertical shafts safely. |
| **Swing Momentum** | Movement Keys | Build side-to-side energy to reach distant platforms. |

> **💡 Tip:** Never check out a ledge until you have secured your grapple. If you trip or overextend, the rope is the only thing preventing an instant game over.

## Survival and Resource Management

While the centipede is your most visible threat, the environment itself is equally lethal. **itch idols of ash** features a health system tied to "Vitality." Taking damage from falls, slamming into walls at high speeds, or being nipped by the centipede reduces this bar. Recovery is rare and usually tied to specific environmental objects.

### Damage Types and Health Recovery

Understanding how much punishment your character can take allows you to make "calculated risks"—leaps of faith that might save time but cost health.

| Damage Source | Severity | Mitigation Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Minor Falls** | Low | Aim for flat ground; avoid jagged rocks. |
| **Wall Collisions** | Medium | Use the "Pull in" mechanic to slow your swing before impact. |
| **Centipede Strike** | High | Keep moving; the centipede deals massive damage upon contact. |
| **Extreme Heights** | Lethal | Always use the grapple; never free-fall more than two "stories." |

Vitality can be regained by finding specific "Spirit Corpses" or wrapped remains scattered throughout the ruins. These often appear near difficult platforming sections, acting as a reward for players who explore the cubbies and hidden alcoves of the megastructure.

## The Stalker: Evading the Giant Centipede

The central antagonist of **itch idols of ash** is a massive, man-eating centipede with disturbing human-like features. This creature is not a scripted event; it is an active hunter that tracks your position and navigates the ruins with terrifying speed.

### Centipede Behavior Patterns

The centipede moves through the environment using the same verticality you do. It can crawl along walls, ceilings, and through the very tree roots you use for cover. 

1.  **The Stalking Phase:** The creature stays at a distance, making clicking and verbalizing noises. This is your signal to keep moving.
2.  **The Sprint:** When the centipede gets within a certain range, it will accelerate. You will hear its breathing intensify. At this point, you must make a "leap of faith" to put distance between you.
3.  **Ambush Points:** Certain narrow corridors are designed to let the centipede trap you. Always look for "outside" routes along the exterior of the building to maintain a lead.

> **⚠️ Warning:** The centipede is faster on flat ground than you are. Your only advantage is your ability to swing into open space where it cannot easily follow without circumnavigating the gap.

## Narrative and the Idols of Ash

The story of the game is told through "Urns" or checkpoints where you "Breathe in the ashes." Each interaction triggers a memory of a woman the protagonist is searching for. These memories paint a picture of a world ravaged by the "Coil Rot" and a protagonist fueled by a desperate, perhaps even delusional, sense of guilt.

### Memory Milestones

As you progress through **itch idols of ash**, the ashes reveal the tragic history of the protagonist's journey.

| Memory Phase | Narrative Context | Gameplay Impact |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **The Village** | The day you left; a sense of duty and regret. | Acts as the first major checkpoint. |
| **The Coil Rot** | Details of the sickness that took "her." | Introduces more complex vertical hazards. |
| **The Distant Shape** | Seeing her figure, beautiful but unreachable. | Signals the final, most dangerous descent. |
| **The Final Idol** | The realization of what the Idols truly are. | Unlocks the ending and Nightmare Mode. |

The ending suggests a cyclical or transformative nature to the ruins. By the time you reach the bottom, the line between the protagonist and the "Idols" of the past becomes blurred. The final idol represents the ultimate face of regret—the realization that some memories are more ruinous than the structures themselves.

## Advanced Tactics for Nightmare Mode

Upon completing the game for the first time, you unlock Nightmare Mode. This mode is designed for players who have mastered the movement in **itch idols of ash**. In this difficulty:
*   The centipede is significantly faster and more aggressive.
*   Vitality recovery is reduced by 50%.
*   Grapple points are fewer, forcing you to use longer swings and more dangerous momentum-based jumps.

To succeed here, you must utilize "Speedrun Strats." This involves intentional free-falling to bypass entire sections of the map, only using the grapple at the last possible second to break your fall. While risky, it is the only way to stay ahead of the Nightmare Centipede.

## FAQ

**Q: Is there a way to kill the centipede in itch idols of ash?**

A: No, the centipede cannot be killed. The game is designed as a pure survival-horror experience where evasion and movement are your only defenses. You must focus on descending as quickly as possible.

**Q: How do I stop taking damage when I land?**

A: To minimize fall damage, try to grapple an object just before you hit the ground and use the "Pull in" function to counteract your downward velocity. Alternatively, landing on slightly sloped surfaces can sometimes reduce the impact compared to flat ground.

**Q: What happens if I miss a checkpoint Urn?**

A: If you bypass an Urn, you will not see that specific piece of lore, and your respawn point will remain at the last Urn you interacted with. It is highly recommended to find every Urn to make the descent more manageable.

**Q: Does itch idols of ash have multiple endings?**

A: The game primarily features one narrative conclusion, but the interpretation of the ending changes based on how much of the lore you uncover through the ash memories. Unlocking Nightmare Mode is considered the "true" challenge for dedicated players.
