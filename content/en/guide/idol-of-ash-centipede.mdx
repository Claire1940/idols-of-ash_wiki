## Mastering Grappling and Movement

The grappling claw is your only tool for survival. It allows you to hook onto specific surfaces, pull yourself in, or swing across wide gaps. However, the physics-based movement means that momentum can be both your best friend and your worst enemy.

### Safety 101: Securing Your Position
One of the most vital rules in *Idols of Ash* is to never check a ledge or attempt a risky maneuver until you have secured yourself with the hook. If you overextend or trip without a line attached, the resulting fall damage is often fatal. By keeping a hook active while scouting, you create a safety net that prevents accidental deaths.

### Advanced Descent Techniques
While the goal is to reach the bottom, the path is rarely linear. You will encounter various terrain types that affect how your hook behaves:

*   **Stone Walls:** Reliable for hooking and swinging.
*   **Tree Roots:** Difficult to hook; the claw may bounce or slide off, making these areas high-risk.
*   **Flat Ground:** Rare, but provides a moment to recover stamina and plan the next move.

| Action | Risk Level | Benefit |
|---|---|---|
| **Short Drops** | Low | Preserves health while maintaining a steady pace. |
| **Long Swings** | Medium | Covers horizontal distance quickly to avoid the **idol of ash centipede**. |
| **Blind Leaps** | High | Can bypass entire sections but risks landing in dead ends or taking fatal damage. |
| **Controlled Rappelling** | Low | The safest way to descend narrow shafts and maintain hook tension. |

> **💡 Tip:** Use "speedrun strats" sparingly. While jumping down large distances deals less damage than expected at certain heights, consistent fall damage will eventually drain your health bar, leaving you vulnerable to the centipede.

## Avoiding the Idol of Ash Centipede

Survival is a game of distance. The **idol of ash centipede** picks up pace the longer you stay in one area. If you hear heavy breathing or the sound of shifting stone directly above you, it is time to move. 

The monster is not just a mindless chaser; it can ambush you. There are specific triggers in the environment—often near narrow pathways or tree roots—where the centipede will attempt to cut you off. When this happens, look for "crazy" shortcuts. Grappling onto distant pillars or taking a calculated fall can create a significant lead, forcing the monster to recalibrate its path.

### Health and Vitality Management
Throughout the ruins, you will find bundles or corpses that grant vitality. These are often placed in dangerous cubbies or on exposed ledges. 
*   **Vitality Pickups:** Look for glowing or wrapped shapes. These restore your health bar.
*   **Strategic Sacrifices:** Sometimes it is better to take a small amount of fall damage to reach a lower platform than to wait for a perfect hook and let the centipede catch up.

## Narrative Checkpoints: The Idols of Ash

As you descend, you will encounter green urns or "Idols of Ash." Interacting with these allows you to "breathe in the ancient ashes," which serves as both a checkpoint and a narrative device. These moments provide glimpses into the protagonist's past and the reason for their descent.

| Memory | Context | Narrative Weight |

| **The Village** | A memory of the day the protagonist left home. | Establishes the sense of loss and purpose. |

| **The Coil Rot** | A vision of a frail, twisted woman suffering from a mysterious ailment. | Reveals the tragic motivation behind the descent. |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not linger at checkpoints for too long. While the game provides a brief moment of respite during the text crawl, the **idol of ash centipede** is often waiting just outside the interaction zone to resume the hunt.

## The Final Descent and Nightmare Mode

The final stretch of the game removes many of the safety nets. You will find fewer urns and more dead ends. The architecture becomes more ruinous and intangible, reflecting the "tricks of the mind" warned about in the lore.

Upon reaching the bottom and interacting with the final idol, the story concludes with a bittersweet revelation. The protagonist, seeking to reunite with a memory, essentially becomes part of the ruins—an idol of ash themselves. This preservation through ash suggests that the entire journey was a ritual of grief and remembrance.

### Unlocking Nightmare Mode
Completing the game for the first time in 2026 unlocks **Nightmare Mode**. This mode increases the aggression of the **idol of ash centipede**, reduces the frequency of vitality pickups, and may alter the layout of the ruins to include more "ruinous illusions" that can lead to fatal falls.

## FAQ

**Q: Can you actually kill the idol of ash centipede?**

A: No. The centipede is an environmental hazard and a psychological pressure mechanic. There is no combat in the game; your only options are evasion, speed, and clever use of the grappling hook.

**Q: How do I handle the sections with tree roots where the hook won't stick?**

A: These sections are designed to be "surf" maps. You must rely on sliding and momentum. Try to aim your hook at the stone structures surrounding the roots rather than the roots themselves to maintain control.

**Q: What happens if the idol of ash centipede catches me?**

A: Contact with the centipede results in an instant "Game Over." You will be restarted at the last urn where you breathed in the ashes.

**Q: Are there multiple endings to the story?**

A: The main ending remains consistent, focusing on the protagonist becoming an idol of ash. However, the dialogue and memories you experience can vary slightly depending on which path you take and which urns you discover during your descent.
