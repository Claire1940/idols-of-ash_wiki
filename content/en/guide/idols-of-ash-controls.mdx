### Grappling and Momentum Physics
The core of the **idols of ash controls** experience is the grappling hook. Unlike other platformers where a grapple is a simple "point and pull" mechanic, here it functions on a pendulum system. Your weight, the length of the rope, and the angle of attachment all dictate your exit velocity.

*   **The Swing Arc:** Aiming higher on a wall allows for a longer swing but slower acceleration. Aiming closer to your horizontal plane results in a "snap" effect that can launch you across gaps.
*   **Momentum Conservation:** Releasing the grapple at the lowest point of your arc provides the most horizontal speed. Releasing at the end of the upward swing provides height but kills your forward drive.

## Navigating Enemy Pressure with Precise Controls

As you progress, the game introduces variants of enemies often referred to by the community as "Kyle," "Kevin," and "Karen." These enemies don't just deal damage; they disrupt your movement flow.

1.  **Kyle Variants:** These enemies track your grappling point. To avoid them, you must frequently "pump" your grapple (releasing and re-attaching) to change your trajectory.
2.  **Kevin Variants:** These provide area-of-effect pressure. Using your **idols of ash controls** to maintain a high-ground position is the only way to avoid their ground-based slams.
3.  **Pressure Management:** When a "Keeper" starts putting pressure on your run, focus on your descent speed. The faster you move downward, the less time the AI has to calculate your path.

> **Warning:** Taking fall damage during a chase is often a death sentence. Always keep your Cape ready when navigating the vertical shafts of the lower Kiln.

## Controller vs. Keyboard: Which is Best?

There is a significant debate in the community regarding the optimal way to engage with the **idols of ash controls**. 

*   **Keyboard & Mouse:** Offers unparalleled aiming precision for the grappling hook. This is essential for "clutch saves" where you need to hook a tiny platform while falling at terminal velocity.
*   **Controller:** Offers smoother 360-degree movement. While the aiming is slightly less precise, the analog stick allows for better "drift" control when using the Cape or Jetpack.

Most players who have successfully cleared the First Kiln suggest using a mouse for the initial learning curve, as the grapple aim is the most frequent cause of death. You can find more community discussions and technical support on the [Idols of Ash Steam Community Hub](https://store.steampowered.com/app/2413370/Idols_of_Ash/).

## Strategic Tips for the First Kiln

The final section of the game is a test of everything you have learned about the **idols of ash controls**. You will often find yourself at low health with enemies right on your heels.

*   **The "Horrific Save":** If you miss a platform, don't panic. Aim your grapple directly beneath you. If there is any geometry at all, the game's physics will often allow for a "slingshot" save that can put you back on track.
*   **Health Management:** There are specific "hills" or checkpoints in the Kiln section. If you can reach these without taking damage from enemies, you can practically refill your health before the final stretch.
*   **The Descent:** Use your momentum to "slide" down slopes. This is faster than jumping and keeps you under the radar of flying variants.

## FAQ

**Q: Why do I lose all my momentum when I release the grapple?**

A: This usually happens if you release the grapple while the rope is under maximum tension at an awkward angle. To maintain speed, try to release the **idols of ash controls** input when your character is at the nadir (lowest point) of the swing arc.

**Q: How do I activate the Cape to avoid fall damage?**

A: By default, you must hold the Shift key (or LB on controller) while in mid-air. Note that the Cape must be purchased or found during your run; it is not a permanent starting item unless unlocked via specific achievements in 2026.

**Q: Can I change the grappling hook sensitivity?**

A: Yes, in the settings menu under "Input," you can adjust both the aim sensitivity and the "tether slack." Increasing the tether slack allows for more bouncy movement, while decreasing it makes the grapple feel more rigid and predictable.

**Q: Are the controls the same for all character variants?**

A: Yes, the core **idols of ash controls** remain consistent across all characters. However, some characters have different weights and gravity scales, which will change the "feel" of your momentum and how long you can hold a swing before the rope snaps.
