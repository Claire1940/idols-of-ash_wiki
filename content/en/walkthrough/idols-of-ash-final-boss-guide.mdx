Conquering the Brume Tower in Dark Souls 2: Crown of the Old Iron King requires more than just combat prowess; it demands a strategic approach to the haunting statues known as Ashen Idols. This **idols of ash final boss guide** will walk you through the intricate process of neutralizing these idols and defeating the legendary Fume Knight. To truly master this DLC, players must understand that the **idols of ash final boss guide** isn't just about a single encounter, but a comprehensive journey to collect the fragments of Nadalia, Bride of Ash. Without the proper preparation and the use of Smelter Wedges, your battle against the final bosses of this region will be significantly more difficult, if not impossible.

## Understanding Ashen Idols and Smelter Wedges

Before you can face the final challenges of the Old Iron King DLC, you must understand the relationship between Smelter Wedges and Ashen Idols. There are 11 Ashen Idols scattered throughout Brume Tower. Each idol contains a fragment of the Soul of Nadalia. To "silence" an idol, you must interact with it while having a Smelter Wedge in your inventory.

You begin the DLC with six Smelter Wedges found on a corpse before crossing the massive chain to the tower. However, to find the remaining wedges and eventually complete the Soul of Nadalia, you will need to explore the deepest reaches of the forge and even venture into the memories of the past.

| Item | Total Quantity | Primary Use |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Smelter Wedge** | 11 | Used to destroy Ashen Idols and stop their area-of-effect buffs. |
| **Soul Fragment** | 12 | 11 from Idols, 1 from Fume Knight. Combines into the True Soul of Nadalia. |
| **Tower Key** | 1 | Required to access several locked areas containing Idols and elevators. |
| **Scorching Iron Scepter** | 1 | Activates the machinery and elevators within Brume Tower. |

> **Warning:** Do not waste your Smelter Wedges early on. While it is tempting to use them on every idol you see, ensure you save at least four for the Fume Knight's arena, or the boss fight will be nearly impossible to sustain.

## Idols of Ash Final Boss Guide: The Fume Knight Strategy

The Fume Knight, also known as Raime, serves as the primary mechanical final boss of the Brume Tower. This encounter is famously difficult because of the four Ashen Idols positioned just outside the boss arena. If these idols are not destroyed, they will radiate a healing aura that restores the Fume Knight's health whenever he stands near the edge of the arena.

### Preparing the Arena

Before passing through the fog gate, you must use four Smelter Wedges on the idols surrounding the pit. If you have run out of wedges, you must backtrack to find the chest containing four additional wedges located in the hallway filled with exploding barrel-carrying hollows.

| Boss Phase | Health Threshold | Key Attacks | Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Phase 1** | 100% - 50% | Straight sword combos and Greatsword sweeps. | Roll toward his smaller sword side; don't get greedy with hits. |
| **Phase 2** | 50% - 0% | Flaming Ultra Greatsword, AoE fire bursts. | High fire resistance is vital. Roll through the slow flame sweeps. |

### Combat Tactics
The Fume Knight deals physical, fire, and dark damage. Wearing the **Velstadt's Helm** will immediately trigger his second phase, which some veteran players prefer because his movements become more predictable, albeit more damaging. For the standard approach, stay behind his left leg and manage your stamina carefully. This **idols of ash final boss guide** recommends using a fast weapon with high counter strength, such as a rapier or a katana, to capitalize on the small windows of opportunity Raime provides.

## Locating Every Ashen Idol

To complete the Soul of Nadalia, you must track down all 11 idols. Many are hidden behind environmental puzzles or guarded by Iron Warriors.

1.  **Entry Way:** Immediately after the first bonfire.
2.  **The Iron Warrior Room:** Located in a room where an Iron Warrior emerges from the floor.
3.  **The Curse Tower:** This idol emits a curse aura. It is highly recommended to wear the **Hollow Skin** mask or the **Cursebite Ring** before approaching.
4.  **The Prowler Gank:** Located at the top of the tower, guarded by several Prowler phantoms.
5.  **The Fume Knight Perimeter:** Four idols located around the final boss arena.

> **Tip:** If you are struggling with the Curse Tower, the Hollow Skin mask found within the DLC provides 1000 resistance to curse, effectively making you immune to the idol's negative effects.

## Reaching the Final Idol: Sir Alonne

The 11th and final Smelter Wedge is not found in Brume Tower itself. Instead, it is a reward for defeating Sir Alonne in the **Memory of the Old Iron King**. To access this memory, you must possess the **Ashen Mist Heart** (obtained from the Ancient Dragon in the base game) and interact with the glowing red armor at the top of the tower.

Sir Alonne is considered one of the fastest and most challenging bosses in the series. Upon his defeat, you can access his throne room to find the final wedge. This wedge is then used on the last Ashen Idol, typically found in the cursed room near the Foyer bonfire.

| Boss: Sir Alonne | Details |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Weakness** | Physical (Slash/Thrust), Magic |
| **Resistance** | Fire, Bleed, Poison |
| **Key Drop** | Soul of Sir Alonne, Final Smelter Wedge |

For more detailed lore on the Old Iron King's fall, you can visit the [official Dark Souls website](https://www.bandainamcoent.com/games/dark-souls-ii) for historical context on the DLC expansions.

## Rewards: The True Soul of Nadalia

Once you have collected all 12 fragments (11 from idols and 1 from the Fume Knight), they will automatically combine into the **True Soul of Nadalia, Bride of Ash**. This soul can be traded with Straid of Olaphis or Weaponsmith Ornifex for powerful end-game items.

*   **Outcry (Pyromancy):** Traded with Straid. It is a superior version of Firestorm that casts faster and deals dark-fire damage.
*   **Chime of Screams:** Traded with Ornifex. A unique chime that increases casting speed but has high faith requirements.

Most players choose **Outcry** as it is one of the most effective Pyromancies for both PvP and PvE encounters in 2026.

## FAQ

**Q: What happens if I run out of Smelter Wedges before the Fume Knight?**

A: You must explore the tower further. There are 11 wedges in total. If you used them all on previous idols and haven't found the chest with four wedges near the barrel-carrying hollows, you will need to retrieve those to clear the idols around the boss.

**Q: Can I beat the Fume Knight without destroying the idols?**

A: It is possible but extremely difficult. The idols will heal him for a significant portion of his health whenever he is near the edge of the arena. This **idols of ash final boss guide** strongly suggests destroying at least the idols on the side where you plan to fight him.

**Q: Where is the 11th Smelter Wedge located?**

A: The final wedge is found in the throne room after defeating Sir Alonne in the Memory of the Old Iron King. You need the Ashen Mist Heart to enter this area.

**Q: Does the Soul of Nadalia carry over to New Game Plus?**

A: The fragments themselves are key items and typically do not carry over, but the completed Soul of Nadalia and the weapons/spells crafted from it will remain in your inventory. Always finish the collection before starting a new journey.
