## Mastering Movement and the Grapple Hook

The core of the gameplay revolves around the grappling hook, a tool that provides both salvation and a swift death depending on your timing. In **dols of ash**, the physics engine allows for significant momentum carry, meaning you can swing through the air at terrifying speeds. This "Monkey Ball" style of movement is essential for bypassing difficult sections, though it increases the risk of fatal impact.

To use the grapple effectively, you must learn to lead your shots. The hook travels at a set velocity, and hitting a moving or distant target requires precise anticipation. Once attached, you can reel yourself in or let out slack to adjust your arc. Mastering the "swing-release" mechanic is the difference between a controlled descent and a frantic tumble into the jaws of the centipede.

| Action | Control (Default) | Tactical Use |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Grapple Fire** | Left Mouse Button | Attach to stone surfaces to arrest a fall. |
| **Reel In** | W / Scroll Up | Pull yourself toward the anchor point quickly. |
| **Release Slack** | S / Scroll Down | Extend the line to create a wider swing arc. |
| **Jump/Release** | Spacebar | Detach from the hook and preserve momentum. |
| **Interact** | E | Read ancient inscriptions or activate beacons. |

> **💡 Tip:** Looking upward while falling can sometimes alter your air control and speed, a quirk often utilized by high-level players to navigate tighter corridors without hitting walls.

## Health Management and Fall Damage

Surviving the depths is not just about avoiding the bottom; it is about managing your physical state. **dols of ash** features a nuanced health system where fall damage is calculated based on vertical velocity and the angle of impact. Small drops will merely chip away at your health, while massive plunges are instantly fatal unless you manage to snag a grapple point at the last possible second.

Health does not regenerate automatically. You must find specific "ancient ash" caches or reach green beacons to restore your vitality. Understanding the thresholds for damage is vital for deciding when to take a "leap of faith" to skip a platforming section.

| Fall Height | Damage Type | Recovery Method |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Short (1-5m)** | Negligible | None required; impact is absorbed. |
| **Medium (6-15m)** | Moderate | Requires **Ancient Ash** consumption. |
| **Long (16-30m)** | Severe | Requires immediate Beacon interaction. |
| **Extreme (30m+)** | Fatal | Instant death; restart from last checkpoint. |

## The Centipede: Survival Strategies

The most polarizing element of **dols of ash** is undoubtedly the giant centipede. This creature serves as both a psychological deterrent and a physical obstacle. It often appears in narrow bottlenecks, forcing you to move quickly or find an alternative route. The sound design is your best warning; the skittering of many legs against stone indicates that the creature is nearby.

When the centipede is active, the game transitions into a high-stakes chase. You cannot fight the creature directly; your only defense is speed and verticality. Use your grapple to stay above the creature's pathing, as it primarily sticks to the walls and floors of the main chasm.

1. **Listen for Audio Cues:** The centipede makes a distinct clicking sound before it enters your field of vision.
2. **Use High Brightness:** While it ruins the atmosphere, increasing your gamma settings in the options menu can help you spot the creature in the darkest corners.
3. **Avoid the Walls:** The centipede is a master of wall-climbing. Staying in the center of the chasm using your grapple is generally safer.
4. **Don't Panic:** Many players die because they release their grapple too early when they hear the creature. Maintain your grip until you have a clear landing zone.

## Speedrunning and Technical Shortcuts

The community for **dols of ash** has discovered that the game is ripe for speedrunning. By exploiting the physics of the grapple, players can skip entire sections of the map. The current world records in 2026 hover around the 4-minute mark, achieved by essentially falling through the center of the map and only grappling at the last second to avoid death.

The "Super Monkey Ball" technique involves building up horizontal velocity by swinging in wide circles. This allows you to launch yourself across massive gaps, bypassing the intended platforming path. However, this is extremely risky, as hitting a wall at these speeds will result in instant death regardless of your current health.

| Shortcut Name | Difficulty | Time Saved | Description |

## Lore and Atmosphere: The Ancient Ashes

As you descend, the game provides snippets of narrative through environmental storytelling and "Dark Souls style" NPC dialogue. You are a traveler who has left your village to breathe in the "ancient ashes" of this place. The game suggests that the descent is a form of purification or perhaps a descent into madness.

The environment shifts from musty, stale caves to surreal landscapes filled with pine trees and morning dew—remnants of the protagonist's memories. These "illusions" are designed to distract you. In **dols of ash**, the more you look at the beauty of the illusions, the faster the centipede approaches. It is a brilliant mechanic that punishes the player for engaging with the game's beauty.

## Navigating the Gates

The game is divided by several massive "gates" or chasms. Each gate acts as a transition point between different biomes of the descent. Reaching these gates often triggers a checkpoint, marked by a green beacon of light.

*   **The Gate of Memory:** The first major drop where illusions begin to manifest.
*   **The Centipede's Nest:** A narrow, vertical tunnel where the creature is most aggressive.
*   **The Final Descent:** A wide-open void where you must rely entirely on your grapple to find the bottom.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I avoid the centipede in the narrow tunnels?**

A: The best strategy is to maintain high momentum. Do not stop to look back. Use short, rapid grapple pulls to "zip" along the ceiling where the centipede has a harder time reaching you.

**Q: What are the green beacons in dols of ash?**

A: These are checkpoints and health restoration points. Once you touch the light, your progress is saved. If you die, you will respawn at the last activated beacon with full health.

**Q: Can I kill the monsters in the game?**

A: No, there is no combat system in the traditional sense. You are completely defenseless and must rely on your movement and environmental awareness to survive the threats you encounter.

**Q: Is there a secret ending?**

A: Yes, players have discovered that reaching the bottom in under 5 minutes without taking any fall damage unlocks a unique "Ascension" cutscene, though it is incredibly difficult to achieve without mastering the speedrun techniques mentioned above.
