## Mastering the Grappling Hook Mechanics

The heart of the gameplay lies in its physics-based grappling system. Unlike traditional platformers where movement is precise and digital, this game utilizes a momentum-heavy rope mechanic that requires a keen understanding of arc and velocity. Players must learn to "feel" the weight of their character as they swing across wide chasms or ascend toward distant ledges.

To excel, you must master the "Full Swing" technique. This involves attaching the hook at the highest possible point and allowing your character to reach maximum downward velocity before releasing. This momentum can then be converted into a long-range jump, allowing you to bypass dangerous sections of the cave floor where the stalker is most likely to catch you.

| Mechanic | Input/Action | Effect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Grapple Attach** | Left Click / Trigger | Connects the rope to any valid stone surface. |
| **Rope Retraction** | Scroll Up / Button | Pulls the player closer to the anchor point for vertical climbing. |
| **Momentum Release** | Release Click | Disconnects the hook, launching the player based on current speed. |
| **Crouch/Slide** | Control Key | Used to dampen impact or slide through narrow crevices. |

> **Warning:** Falling from heights greater than 30 feet will result in "broken legs," significantly slowing your movement speed and making you an easy target for the creature. Always aim for a ledge or a lower grapple point to break your fall.

## Navigating the Stalker and Environmental Hazards

Survival in the depths is not just about platforming; it is about evasion. The primary antagonist—a fast, multi-limbed horror often referred to as the "Murderpede"—is constantly tracking your position. This creature does not follow a set path but reacts to the noise you make and your proximity to the ground.

Environmental hazards also play a significant role. The "Coil Rot" and the "Ancient Ash" mentioned in the game's narrative prompts aren't just lore; they represent the decaying state of the world you are traversing. You will encounter "Idols" or shrines throughout the cave which serve as checkpoints. Activating these is vital, as they provide a brief moment of respite and a save point should you fall victim to the stalker.

## Difficulty Modes: From Normal to Burst Clean

For players looking for a true challenge in 2026, the game offers several tiers of difficulty. Each tier modifies the AI behavior of the stalker and the availability of resources.

1.  **Normal Mode:** The standard experience. The stalker is persistent but manageable, and checkpoints are frequent.
2.  **Nightmare Mode:** The enemy is significantly "faster and smarter." Damage taken is increased, and many of the protective idols have been destroyed, leaving you with fewer safety nets.
3.  **Burst Clean:** The ultimate test of skill. This mode requires a near-perfect run through the "Viper's Pit" and other late-game areas. It is designed for players who have completely mastered the grappling hook's physics.

> **Tip:** In Nightmare mode, the stalker can predict your landing zones. Avoid staying in the air too long in a predictable arc, as it may be waiting for you where you land.

## Pro Tips for Advanced Play

To survive the hardest levels, you must move like "the next Peter Parker." This requires more than just swinging; it requires environmental awareness.

*   **Juke Maneuvers:** When the stalker is directly beneath you, use a quick vertical pull to change your elevation suddenly. This often resets the creature's pathfinding.
*   **Ledge Grabbing:** You don't always need to land on flat ground. You can grapple the underside of a ledge and use the "Rope Retraction" to pull yourself up into a safe nook.
*   **Audio Cues:** Listen for the skittering sounds of the stalker. The game uses spatial audio to tell you exactly how close the threat is. If the skittering stops, it likely means the creature is preparing to pounce.

| Advanced Technique | Execution | Benefit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **The Slingshot** | Grapple far ahead and swing low | Generates massive horizontal speed to outrun the stalker. |
| **The Anchor Drop** | Release grapple and immediately crouch | Minimizes the "stumble" animation upon landing. |
| **Ceiling Camping** | Stay attached to high ceilings | Keeps you out of the stalker's reach while you plan your next move. |
