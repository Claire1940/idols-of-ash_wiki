## Standard Mode: The Descent into Memory

Standard mode serves as the narrative foundation for the game. In this mode, players assume the role of a traveler searching for a lost loved one within an ancient, musty structure. The primary mechanic involves a grappling hook used to swing, slide, and reel through vertical environments.

As you descend, you will encounter glowing altars containing ancient ashes. Inhaling these ashes provides a temporary reprieve from the stale air, replacing it with the scent of pine and morning dew. However, these ashes are hallucinogenic, manifesting ghostly outlines of a village and a woman from the protagonist's past.

### Grappling Hook Mechanics

| Action | Control (PC) | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Throw Claw** | Left Click | Attaches to any valid stone or wood surface. |
| **Reel In Rope** | Shift (Hold) | Pulls the player toward the anchor point; stops momentum. |
| **Slide Down** | Space / Control | Allows for a controlled descent while attached. |
| **Jump/Detach** | Space | Releases the hook to allow for momentum-based swinging. |

In Standard mode, the giant centipede—a creature reminiscent of the prehistoric *Arthropleura*—will chase you periodically. Use the environment's verticality to stay out of its reach. If you take damage, look for "Kindles" (latent embers) to rekindle your vitality.

## Nightmare Mode: No Room for Error

Once you have completed the story, Nightmare mode unlocks, significantly altering the experience. This is the most punishing of the **idols of ash modes**, designed for players who have mastered the "speedrun tech" of the grappling hook.

1.  **Kilns are Destroyed:** The altars that previously served as checkpoints no longer save your progress. A single death sends you back to the very beginning of the descent.
2.  **Increased Aggression:** The centipede is not only faster but can predict player movement more effectively, making it harder to "cheese" the monster by staying on thin branches.
3.  **Vitality Scarcity:** While Kindles still exist, the damage taken from falls or enemy contact is much higher, often resulting in a two-hit kill.

Survival in Nightmare mode requires "momentum stopping." By holding the reel-in button (Shift) mid-air, you can cancel your downward velocity to avoid lethal fall damage. This technique is vital when navigating the narrow shafts near the game's midpoint.

## First Kill Moon: The Ultimate Trial

The "First Kill Moon" is a bonus map described as a "place of trials and despair." Unlike the main **idols of ash modes**, this map does not offer narrative closure or answers. Instead, it serves as a pure mechanical challenge.

In this mode, the environment is pixelated and distorted, and players are pursued by multiple centipede entities simultaneously. There is no stalling for time here; the speed of the pursuit is so high that even a single missed grapple results in immediate failure. Players often refer to this as the "true" horror experience, as it removes the comforting hallucinations of the protagonist's wife and replaces them with an endless cycle of pursuit.

## The Lore of Petrification

A recurring theme across all **idols of ash modes** is the concept of turning to stone. The story suggests that the ancient structure is filled with minerals like calcium carbonate and magnesium—similar to real-world locations like Mother Shipton's Cave. 

While science explains the slow calcification of objects over months, the game's magical ash accelerates this process. The protagonist’s desire to see his deceased wife leads him to inhale lethal amounts of hallucinogens, eventually causing him to succumb to the environment. Every statue you see during your descent represents a previous traveler who chose a beautiful lie over a harsh reality, eventually being "encrusted" by the cave's minerals.

> **Warning:** Falling too far without using your grapple to reset momentum will result in "broken ankles," a state that significantly reduces your movement speed and makes you easy prey for the centipede.

## Expert Survival Tips for 2026

*   **Sensitivity Calibration:** Ensure your mouse sensitivity is high enough to perform 180-degree turns mid-fall. This allows you to hook onto platforms you have already passed.
*   **Abuse the Walls:** You can grapple and reel into vertical walls to "wall-run" or stabilize yourself before a big jump.
*   **Ignore the Memories:** While the ghostly outlines are tempting to follow for lore, they often lead to dead ends. Focus on the high beams of light that indicate the next ash altar.
*   **The Centipede’s Sound:** The creature makes a distinct skittering and breathing sound. If the audio becomes localized to one side, it means the monster is on the wall next to you, not just above you.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I unlock the different idols of ash modes?**

A: Standard mode is available from the start. Nightmare mode is unlocked after reaching the final altar in Standard. The "First Kill Moon" bonus map typically unlocks after completing a run in Nightmare mode or by finding a hidden Kindle in the final abyss.

**Q: Can you actually kill the giant centipede?**

A: No. In all current **idols of ash modes**, the centipede is an invincible environmental hazard. Your only defense is speed, verticality, and the grappling hook.

**Q: What do the Kindles do in Nightmare mode?**

A: In Nightmare mode, Kindles are your only source of healing. Since there are no checkpoints, maintaining high vitality is the only way to survive the increased damage modifiers of the late-game sections.

**Q: Is there a secret ending in First Kill Moon?**

A: Currently, First Kill Moon is considered a "trial" mode with no narrative ending. It is designed to test mechanical skill rather than provide story resolution, though some players speculate that a perfect run may reveal additional lore fragments in the future.
